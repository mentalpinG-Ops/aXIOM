## Context

First informal presentation of the aXIOM project to academics and artists
in the facilitator's personal network. No working build available. 
Presentation based on slides (stakeholder deck) and verbal description.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Institutional benefit argument ineffective
The group did not respond to arguments framed around institutional or
departmental utility. Individual benefit — what the tool does for *me,
in my practice* — is the relevant frame for this audience profile.

> **Implication:** COMMS.md framing must foreground teacher-level utility,
not institutional compliance or efficiency.

### 2. Artefact analysis preferred over academic assessment
Participant interest centred on the analytical capability of the tool
(Research Analyser / Modules 2–3) rather than the assessment function
(Module 4). The group showed stronger engagement with the idea of
analysing their own texts and artefacts than with evaluating student work.

> **Strategic note:** This group may be better suited as pilots for
Module 2/3 (Artefact Analyser) than Module 4 (Academic Assessor).
To be assessed before next session.

### 3. Multi-category analysis question raised
Specific question from participant: can one artefact be analysed under
different categories simultaneously, each yielding a different perspective?
Example given: a photograph analysed as medium/composition vs. as
content/meaning.

> **Implication:** Schema should support explicit mode/perspective
switching per artefact. Already partially supported via §0 Methodological
Declaration — needs clearer UI expression.

### 4. Own-text use case surfaced
One participant expressed interest in using the tool to analyse their
own academic writing. This is outside Module 4 scope but relevant to
Research Analyser.

> **Status:** Out of scope for v1.0 — flagged for roadmap.

### 5. Live demo or concrete output required
Abstract framing without concrete output examples had limited persuasive
impact. Facilitator needed to search for examples spontaneously.

> **Action:** Future sessions require a live demo or a concrete aXIOM
output example — slides alone are insufficient for this audience.

### 6. Group format insufficient for onboarding
Group dynamics surfaced collective orientations but suppressed individual
sense-making. The tool's utility is embedded in individual professional
practice.

> **Decision:** 1:1 onboarding required for all future sessions.
Methodological rationale: access to the Lebenswelt of the individual
assessor requires a 1:1 setting (Schütz: first-order constructs only
accessible at the level of individual experience).
