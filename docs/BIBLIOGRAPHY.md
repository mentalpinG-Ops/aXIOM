## I. Design Science Research

- **DSR-01** ✅  
  Hevner, A. R., March, S. T., Park, J., & Ram, S. (2004). Design science in information systems research. *MIS Quarterly*, 28(1), 75–105. https://doi.org/10.2307/25148625  
  *Role in project: Frames aXIOM-als-Produkt as a DSR artifact contribution. Load-bearing reference for the epistemological position. Not OA; full PDF available via ResearchGate and Semantic Scholar. Note: introduced by Claude, not discovered by X1 — presenting it as prior position would be dishonest.*

## II. Phenomenology & Interpretive Methodology

- **PHEN-01** ⚠️  
  Husserl, E. (1970). *The crisis of European sciences and transcendental phenomenology* (D. Carr, Trans.). Northwestern University Press. (Original work published 1936)  
  *Role in project: Source for Lebenswelt concept. Relevant to Discovery phase as elicitation of lived assessment practice. Caution: name-dropping Husserl in the Błocian context is inappropriate given her Cassirer focus.*

- **PHEN-02** ⚠️  
  Schütz, A., & Luckmann, T. (1973). *The structures of the life-world* (R. M. Zaner & H. T. Engelhardt Jr., Trans.). Northwestern University Press.  
  *Role in project: Operationalises Lebenswelt for social science. Grounds the ethnographic strand of the Discovery phase. Same name-dropping caveat as PHEN-01.*

## III. Qualitative & Ethnographic Methods

- **QUAL-01** ⚠️  
  Flick, U. (2018). *An introduction to qualitative research* (6th ed.). SAGE Publications.  
  *Role in project: Methodological anchor for Discovery phase protocol (teilnehmende Beobachtung, Gruppendiskussion, interview methods).*

- **QUAL-02** ⚠️  
  Bohnsack, R. (2014). *Rekonstruktive Sozialforschung: Einführung in qualitative Methoden* (9th ed.). Barbara Budrich.  
  *Role in project: Documentary method and Gruppendiskussion in the German Kulturwissenschaft tradition. Relevant if publication targets German-language venue.*

## IV. Human-Centred & Participatory Design

- **HCD-01** ⚠️  
  Schuler, D., & Namioka, A. (Eds.). (1993). *Participatory design: Principles and practices*. Lawrence Erlbaum Associates.  
  *Role in project: Foundational text for participatory design — designing with users, not for them.*

## V. Communities of Practice & Learning Theory

- **COP-01** ⚠️  
  Wenger, E. (1998). *Communities of practice: Learning, meaning, and identity*. Cambridge University Press.  
  *Role in project: More appropriate than Learning by Design for describing X1's project-level activity, because X1 operates outside institutional contexts.*

- **LBD-01** ⚠️  
  Kalantzis, M., & Cope, B. (2012). *Literacies*. Cambridge University Press.  
  *Role in project: Learning by Design framework — four knowledge processes (experiencing, conceptualising, analysing, applying). Currently in latent evaluation phase. Describes X1's work descriptively (Ordnungsprinzip) but does not guide it normatively. Cannot be anchored in project foundations because it describes institutional pedagogical contexts.*

- **SCAF-01** ⚠️  
  Wood, D., Bruner, J. S., & Ross, G. (1976). The role of tutoring in problem solving. *Journal of Child Psychology and Psychiatry*, 17(2), 89–100. https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1469-7610.1976.tb00381.x  
  *Role in project: Concept of instructional scaffolding. Theoretical basis for treating AI as a scaffold that recedes as student knowledge grows.*

- **BLOOM-01** ⚠️  
  Anderson, L. W., & Krathwohl, D. R. (Eds.). (2001). *A taxonomy for learning, teaching, and assessing: A revision of Bloom's Taxonomy of Educational Objectives*. Longman.  
  *Role in project: Cognitive-process framework underlying the Research Framework escalation logic.*

## VI. Semiological Framework

- **SEM-01** 🔶  
  Barthes, R. (1972). *Mythologies*. Hill and Wang. (Original work published 1957)  
  *Role in project: Concept of myth as second-order semiological system. Theoretical framework for the Artefact Analyser case study (Appendix B of Research Framework). Also relevant to Essay No 1 thesis development.*

## VII. AI Ethics & Citation Integrity

- **AIE-01** ⚠️  
  Hicks, M. T., Humphries, J., & Slater, J. (2024). ChatGPT is bullshit. *Ethics and Information Technology*, 26, 38. https://doi.org/10.1007/s10676-024-09775-5  
  *Role in project: LLMs as structurally indifferent to truth (Frankfurt's bullshit concept). Philosophical foundation for epistemic warnings in Research Framework.*

- **AIE-02** ⚠️  
  UNESCO. (2023). *Guidance for generative AI in education and research*. United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization. https://unesdoc.unesco.org/ark:/48223/pf0000386693  
  *Role in project: International policy framework for human-centred AI use in research. Institutional backdrop.*

- **AIE-03** ⚠️  
  Mollick, E. R., & Mollick, L. (2023). Assigning AI: Seven approaches for students, with prompts. *arXiv preprint arXiv:2306.10052*. https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.4475995  
  *Role in project: Typology of AI roles in academic learning. Informs five-stage escalation model in Research Framework.*

- **AIE-04** ⚠️  
  Mollick, E. R., & Mollick, L. (2023). Using AI to implement effective teaching strategies in classrooms: Five strategies, including prompts. *SSRN Working Paper*. https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.4391243  
  *Role in project: Evidence-based teaching strategies enhanced by AI. Informs Research Framework prompt hygiene rules.*

## VIII. AI Citation Hallucination — Empirical Evidence

- **HAL-01** ⚠️  
  Chelli, M., Descamps, J., Lavoue, V., Trojani, C., Azar, M., Deckert, M., Raynier, J. L., Clowez, G., Boileau, P., & Ruetsch-Chelli, C. (2024). Hallucination rates and reference accuracy of ChatGPT and Bard for systematic reviews: Comparative analysis. *Journal of Medical Internet Research*, 26, e53164. https://doi.org/10.2196/53164  
  *Role in project: Empirical hallucination rates (28.6%–91.3%). Supports citation verification protocol.*

- **HAL-02** ⚠️  
  Linardon, J., Jarman, H. K., McClure, Z., Anderson, C., Liu, C., & Messer, M. (2025). Influence of topic familiarity and prompt specificity on citation fabrication in mental health research using large language models: Experimental study. Deakin University, School of Psychology.  
  *Role in project: 56% citation fabrication rate in GPT-4o. Supports universal verification principle.*

- **HAL-03** ⚠️  
  Kobak, D., Goncharov, A., Bhoomreddy, M., Hair, C., Choong, T. H., & Vines, H. (2023). Fabrication and errors in the bibliographic citations generated by ChatGPT. *Scientific Reports*, 13, 14045. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-023-41032-5  
  *Role in project: Multi-disciplinary study — 55% fabrication in GPT-3.5, 18% in GPT-4. Empirical basis for "never trust, always verify" principle.*

## IX. Knowledge Commons & Surveillance Capitalism

- **KC-01** 🔶  
  Grassmuck, V. (2002/2004). *Freie Software: Zwischen Privat- und Gemeineigentum*. Bundeszentrale für politische Bildung (BPB).  
  *Role in project: Knowledge commons framing. Informs X1's anti-vendor-lock-in values and self-hosted infrastructure decisions (NAS, Gitea, Syncthing).*

- **KC-02** 🔶  
  Zuboff, S. (2019). *The age of surveillance capitalism: The fight for a human future at the new frontier of power*. PublicAffairs.  
  *Role in project: Reference frame for private data infrastructure design decisions. Not directly cited in project documents but shapes the normative position on data sovereignty.*
