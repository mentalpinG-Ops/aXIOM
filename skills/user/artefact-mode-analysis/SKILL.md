# Skill: Artefact Mode Analysis

Perform a structured interpretive or philosophical analysis of any artefact
using the aXIOM schema. Populate each field as instructed. Do not skip
sections; mark genuinely inapplicable fields N/A with a brief reason.

---

## §0 — Declare analytical stance before proceeding

   - analytical_framework: state the interpretive lens
   - analytical_mode: interpretive | philosophical | mixed
   - analyst_type and analyst_id
   - known_analyst_limitations

---

## §1 — Artefact identification and surface description

   - source_type, title, date, origin, medium
   - physical or formal properties relevant to interpretation
   - first_impression: initial reading before analysis begins

---

## §2 — Contextual and intertextual mapping

   - audience_assumptions: what prior knowledge does the artefact presuppose?
   - exclusions: who is implicitly othered or excluded?
   - production_context: institutional, material, and ideological conditions
   - notable_absences: what is conspicuously not said or shown?
   - intertextuality: how does the artefact position itself relative to other texts?

---

## §3 — Claim extraction

   For each claim extracted, record:
   - claim_id, claim_text, claim_type, modality, verifiability
   - evidence_basis and confidence

---

## §4 — Derived claims and inference mapping

   For each derived claim, record:
   - inference_id, source_claim_ids, derived_claim_text
   - inference_type (select from taxonomy below)
   - confidence and basis

   Inference type taxonomy:
   - inference:rhetorical    — persuasive or argumentative strategy implied
   - inference:semiotic      — sign relation or symbolic coding
   - inference:intentional   — agent likely intended X (requires evidence basis)
   - inference:structural    — systemic or institutional pattern implied
   - inference:contextual    — meaning shifts given surrounding context
   - inference:evaluative    — epistemic status assessment
   - inference:network       — artefact connects to broader claim ecosystem
   - inference:historical    — implies or contradicts established record
   - inference:indexical     — sign points to or constitutes its referent
                               through direct connection (physical, causal,
                               or participatory); use when the artefact or
                               one of its elements designates its object by
                               contiguity or co-presence, not by resemblance
                               or convention
   - inference:abductive     — inference to the best explanation; the analyst
                               observes a pattern, tension, or structural
                               feature and proposes the hypothesis that best
                               accounts for it

---

## §5 — Epistemic and evaluative assessment

   - internal_consistency
   - interpretive_grounding (mode:interpretive / mode:philosophical)
   - epistemic_effect: closure_effect and power_effect
   - limitations_of_this_analysis

---

## §6 — Normalization notes

   Apply standard normalization rules: ISO 8601 dates, contemporary place
   names, preserve loaded terms verbatim with [LOADED_TERM] flag.

---

## §7 — Interpretive synthesis checklist

   Complete all fields below before generating the Zotero tag list.

   - Dominant reading: the preferred or intended meaning
   - Oppositional reading: the reading that resists or inverts the dominant
   - Negotiated reading: where the audience partially accepts and partially
     resists
   - Modality structure: how different semiotic modes interact
   - Rhetorical structure: how the argument or appeal is organised
   - Ideological operation: select from the typology below and explain
     HOW it operates in this specific artefact — mechanism, not just label.

     naturalisation  — presents contingent arrangements as natural or
                       inevitable; renders the constructed invisible
     legitimation    — frames power or hierarchy as justified, rational,
                       or authoritative
     mystification   — produces meaning-effects through deliberate
                       indeterminacy; semantic emptying creates a
                       projection surface without specifying content
     saturation      — overloads a sign with meanings to the point
                       where no single reading stabilises; distinction
                       from mystification: saturation adds, mystification
                       removes
     reification     — treats social relations or processes as fixed
                       things; abstractions become objects
     projection      — implicates the audience as co-producer of meaning;
                       the artefact's significance is completed by the
                       viewer's response (mirrors, interactive structures,
                       open works)
     none salient    — no dominant ideological operation detected;
                       state why

   - Affective register and mobilisation strategy
   - Affective structure: classify the primary viewer relation the artefact
     produces. Select one; note if two coexist and explain the tension.
     distancing      — viewer is positioned as observer of something
                       external; the artefact produces contemplation,
                       projection, or sublimation
     immersive       — viewer is drawn into the artefact's world or logic;
                       boundaries between viewer and object weaken
     confrontational — viewer is directly addressed, implicated, or
                       reflected back; the artefact makes the viewer
                       part of its content (mirror logic, accusation,
                       direct address)

   - Collect all tags from §§0–6 into a deduplicated list grouped by prefix
